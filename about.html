About Me

A little about myself...

BIO

All around creative, encapuslating everything from designing cutting edge hardware and software to self expression through written word and visual arts. Highly motivated learner seeking to create novel and beautiful technology for the greater good!

Tucker-Davis Technologies

Software Engineer

At TDT, I created high level components for a Windows application called "Synapse". These components allowed neuroscientists to better design experiments and collect data. I spent most of my time coding in C++ using the Qt framework, but occasionally I would complete a wide range of in-house programming tasks by coding in Python and JavaScript. Sometimes, I would even cross-compile code to be tested in embedded linux devices.

Dycap Media Solutions

Software Engineer

During my last years at University of Florida, I joined a start-up venture with my friends called Dycap, an audio-visual equipment company that sold a revolutionary product called "PerfectTrack". This software enabled the hands-free tracking of subjects for use with remote controlled cameras. I was responsible for the user experience and system design, leveraging my knowledge in Linux, Python, and embedded systems.

NASA Langley Research Center

Intern

As an intern at NASA, I had the privilege of meeting astronauts and working with aeronautics engineers! Under my mentor, I helped document and test a satellite software framework called Core Flight System for use in an unmanned aerial vehicle research mission. I coded in C and Python on a BeagleBone Black embedded system.

Tectron Engineering

Student Engineer

Tectron is a company that manufactures metal detectors for industrial use. University of Florida offers a program called Integrated Product and Process Design, which is a year long class that allows students to get hands on experience with real products. I worked with a team of fellow students to update the company's metal detector design to allow remote communication.
